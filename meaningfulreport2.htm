Senior Project

WakeVibes Co. Ecommerce Store

November 29, 2020

Kyle Williams

Most Meaningful Objective Report
 


 

For this project I had chosen to work on is going to be an online store. I plan to provide a product and a service within the website for customers. The website will be an online store selling wakeboards and wake-surf boards. I also plan to implement a form within the site allowing the customers to request information on having a custom board built. My main goal is to create an easy to navigate site showcasing all the companys surf boards in stock as well as other merchandise accessories like apparel, parts, and boat accessories. I also want to create a separate page within the website where customers can fill out certain criteria regarding having a custom board built for them. I plan to make this website one that stands out with content and media that catches the eye. Making a website that is easy to navigate is one of my main priorities. One of the challenges that I am going to face is getting the content set up to where it looks presentable. Creating the ordering aspect of the site will also be somewhat of a challenge.

Initially my plan was to do all the website with just straight HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. As I started to work on my project, I quickly realized that I had a lot of learning to do and might have bitten off more than I could chew. I knew switching to WordPress was still going to be a challenge because I was still very unfamiliar with each of the methods because it is just not what I do or am comfortable with. My first steps were to decide what I wanted to sell and how I wanted the website to look. I knew I wanted it to be simple but not just simple, I wanted it to be easy on the eyes and easy to navigate. Once I decided on the general theme and layout of the site, I started off by doing a lot of research on how I could make this work the way I envisioned it. My first focus was setting up the Nav bar because to me the site kind of revolves around it. Once I decided on the different pages that I wanted to be in my site I started to move the actual content that would live on each of those pages. My next focus was getting the home page to where I liked it. I started off by making sure my Nav Bar was consistent within the whole site and that my page flips worked accordingly. Then instead of moving onto the main content of the home page I went to working on a footer that was also informative and consistent between pages. Once those were taken care of, I felt like I sort of had a basis for the rest of the website.

I then started to create the desired content and media within the home page along with other elements like featured products, information about the company, and signup forms. Next, I moved onto the About page the Contact page because I had a pretty good idea of how I wanted them to be. I wanted more of pleasant look for the About page that really drew customers in. For the Contact us page I wanted a very straightforward and sort of out there look to make sure the appropriate information is seen. Next, I focused on getting all my products into one location as well as separate locations for different categories. Once I had everything that I wanted to sell in the right location I moved on to making sure that the order process and check out process were as simple as the could be for the customer. Once the site was to the desired functionality, I begin to work on the small stuff like fonts, colors, images, spacing etc. Site usability is more important to me than aesthetics, mainly because I am not a graphical or artistic person at all, but I know when a website contains too much extra elements where it becomes hard to find anything or get anything accomplished. Once I decided that the website was complete, I went through every aspect of it and inspected as well as testing for functionality. I assessed the site and made any necessary adjustments that I felt needed.

I always knew that there is a lot of work that goes into creating a website, but I guess I never really paid attention to the small details that are involved. So much goes into almost every little aspect of a website that most people have no idea about. Most people dont even pay attention to a websites footer but once you start to create one you realize that you could edit it forever and add so many different things to it. I found myself diving deeper and deeper into what you can do with each element or section of a website. One of the biggest things that I learned from these activities is how much happens on the backend of any published website. From payment methods to security concerns, it is nice to see an appealing website but so much goes on that you cant see. I also learned about how detailed you can get when it comes to a single element. What I mean by that is if you take a simple column or row within a page, the effects and positioning are almost endless. Sometimes that became overwhelming with not really being an artsy person like that.

Overall, I am glad that I chose to take on a project like this because it was outside of my comfort zone and I knew it might become challenging, but it was good to learn something new. A lot of research went into completing this project and I would sometimes get lost in finding out how much you can really do to create an effective website. I know I am far for being an expert in this area and Im sure my site has many flaws, but I feel completely comfortable with taking on a task like this sometime in the future if I ever had to. I went into this project completely blind on the process other than some basic HTML and JavaScript knowledge and felt like I gained a tremendous amount from taking this on. I am glad I tried something new although it created some long sleepless and frustrating nights for me.
